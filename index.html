THINK BEFORE YOU CLICK!

Learn how phishing attacks work, recognize fake emails and websites, and stay safe online.

What is Phishing?

Cyber Attack

Phishing is a cyber attack used to steal sensitive information.

Fake Emails

Attackers send fake emails pretending to be trusted companies.

Steal Passwords

Victims unknowingly share passwords, OTPs, and banking details.

Fake Websites

Duplicate login pages are used to collect user information.

Types of Phishing

Email Phishing

Fake emails pretending to be banks or companies.


Example

"An email claiming to be from SBI asking users to verify account details through a malicious link."


⚠ Collects login IDs, passwords, and bank details.

Smishing

Fraudulent SMS messages used to steal information.


Example

"Congratulations! You won a lottery. Share your bank details to claim the prize."



⚠ Tricks users into sharing personal information.

Vishing

Fake calls pretending to be bank officials.



Example

"A fake HDFC customer-care call asking for OTP to unblock ATM card."



⚠ Fraudulent calls used to steal OTPs and passwords.

Spear Phishing

Targeted attacks on specific people.



Example

"An email appearing to come from a senior officer requesting urgent payment details."


⚠ Very dangerous because attackers use personal information.

Whaling

Attacks targeting high-level executives.



Example

"A forged email sent to a CEO requesting fund transfer authorization."


⚠ Targets important business officials.

Clone Phishing

Exact copies of legitimate emails with malicious links.


Example

"A fake IRCTC ticket confirmation email containing a harmful link."


⚠ Malicious links look genuine and trustworthy.

Fake vs Real Email

❌ Fake Email

Suspicious links, spelling mistakes, urgent messages.

✅ Real Email

Official sender, professional format, secure domain.

How to Recognize Phishing Emails

Suspicious Links

Hover before clicking links.

Spelling Mistakes

Many phishing emails contain errors.

Unknown Sender & Anti-Phishing Tools

Check sender emails carefully and use anti-phishing tools for protection.

Urgent Messages

Scammers create fear and urgency.

How to Identify Fake Websites

❌ Fake Website

Wrong spelling, fake login page, suspicious design.

✅ Real Website

HTTPS security, official domain, trusted design.

Social Engineering Tactics

Create Fear

"Your account will be blocked!"

Offer Rewards

"You won ₹10 lakh!"

Pretend Authority

Fake bank officials or support staff.

Manipulate Emotions

Attackers use urgency and excitement.

Real-World Phishing Examples

🏦 Fake SBI KYC Scam

“Your SBI account will be blocked. Update KYC immediately.”

🎁 Lottery Scam

“Congratulations! You won ₹10 lakh.”

Best Practices & Safety Tips

✅ Never click unknown links
✅ Use strong passwords
✅ Enable MFA
✅ Verify sender email
✅ Never share OTP
✅ Install antivirus software
